Natural Park 'Sierra Tejeda-Almijara'

Looking for a walking holiday in Spain? Just a short drive east of Málaga, you’ll find La Axarquía, one of Andalusia’s most unspoilt regions. Nestled at the foot of the Sierra Tejeda and Almijara Natural Park, this area is a paradise for nature lovers, hikers, and anyone seeking peace away from the crowds.

The landscape is breathtakingly diverse: dramatic mountain peaks, fertile valleys, Mediterranean forests and a botanical richness that makes every season unique and colourful.

Scattered among the hills are historic whitewashed villages with Moorish influence, where narrow streets wind between houses decorated with flowers. Here, vineyards, olive and almond groves grow alongside mango, avocado, orange, and lemon trees – a true reflection of southern Spain’s abundance.

The Natural Park offers walking routes for every level, from easy trails to challenging mountain hikes. Outside the hot summer months, the climate is ideal for outdoor activities – usually 10 to 15 degrees warmer than northern Europe, even in winter. Whether you prefer a gentle stroll or a full-day adventure, you’ll be rewarded with stunning views, rich flora and fauna, and the warm hospitality of the locals.

What makes walking here special is the tranquillity: despite the immensity of the park, you’ll often find yourself alone on the trails, surrounded only by silence and nature. You can access some beautiful routes directly from Casa Las Jaras, far from mass tourism but close enough to explore at your own pace.

Bring your binoculars, lace up your boots, and discover the authentic Andalusian countryside – right on your doorstep at Casa Las Jaras.
